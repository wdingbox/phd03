Possibility of Error
  1. error: the inscribers wrongly thought the turtle had six legs.
  2. misprint: accidentally uninteneted.
  3. fake: professional traders made it on purpose on real bones to increase its price.
Error Analysis
  1. The misprint in OBI is less likely. Because oracle bone inscription underwent a complicated process from design, approval, preparation, manufactoring, implementation and finished in religious ritual. Each OBC was expensive and made prudentially with fear.
  2. The same error occurs twice in OBI. The same erro also can be found in bronze. Also the rear legs of the turtle in some bronze characters are very similar to those in scarab in hieroglyphics. These indicates that the error is not accidental but legally approved with a certain reason. 
Error Reasoning
  1. The rear legs of the turtle in bronze are very similar to those in scarab.
  2. Some turtles in bronzes have flying formate similar to the scarab in hieroglyphics.
  3. The six legs of turtle came from the influence of the six legs of scarab in hieroglyphics. 
  4. Scarab is sacred in Egypt. When spreading to China, it turned to be turtle.
  5. The error of six legs of turtle exposed a connection between OBC and Hieroglyphics.
  6. The idea of using turtle bone very likely triggered by the secred scarabe from Egypt
