An old fisherman caught a 'strange turtle', unexpectedly a treasure of more than 3.000 years

VTC News
12/06/2023


In 2003, the image of an old man living in Luoyang city, Henan province (China) fishing on the banks of the Anyang River was very familiar to people in the region. This is his hobby after retirement. Therefore, he often sat by the riverside almost all day.

However, one day, the old man suddenly caught a big fish after lowering the hook. It took him a lot of work to pull this animal ashore. But it's not fish. From a distance, it resembles a large turtle. However, after looking closely, the old man discovered that the turtle was not moving. It is actually an iron or copper block in the shape of a turtle and the special thing is that there are four short arrows on the shell.

In addition, on the back of this animal there are ancient letters. The old man did not know what the ancient writing on the back of the turtle was, but assumed it could be an abandoned ornament. Because it was in the river, it was rusted.

After pondering, the old man finally brought the metal turtle he caught home and put it in a corner.

In the same year 2003, when he learned about the Cultural Heritage Department's recovery of lost cultural treasures and relics, he brought a turtle caught in the river to ask experts to appraise it.

As soon as they saw the turtle that the old man brought, experts surmised that it was no ordinary object. The animal's eyes were wide open, the limbs stretched back, vividly depicting panic.

After careful examination, experts determined that this is a gourd (of the genus softshell turtle), a bronze object made in the late Shang Dynasty (about 1766 BC - 1122 BC). The inscription on this animal's carapace is Armor Bone, a typeface of the Shang Dynasty.

The curious old man asked the experts: "How much is this bronze turtle worth?"

An expert listened to the old man's question, thought for a moment, and then replied: “This is an antique from the hospital, currently worth about 1,8 billion yuan (equivalent to nearly 6.000 billion dong). Your fishing rod has caught 1,8 billion yuan."

When the old man heard it, he was surprised, only a "copper block" of rust has such a price in the sky. This is beyond the imagination of this old man who enjoys fishing every day.

However, the expert's next sentence brought the old man back to reality. That's because this bronze cultural relic is extremely valuable. The inscriptions on it are useful to experts in studying the history of the Shang. Therefore, experts hope that the old man can hand over this artifact. Naturally, the Cultural Heritage Department will have a certain amount of money for the old man.

After hearing this and conducting negotiations, the old man voluntarily handed over the bronze item dating back at least 3.000 years to this agency.

The old man was very happy to have found such a rare treasure. Currently, the statue of a peacock with arrows stuck in its shell is on display at the National Museum of China.

After recovering and deciphering the inscriptions on this bronze medallion, experts have found the origin of the statue and a true historical story of the Shang Dynasty. Accordingly, the inscription on the body of the statue shows information about the hunting ritual in the time of Chu Vuong, the last king of the Shang Dynasty.

Tru Vuong loves to hunt and often goes to the area near the An Duong River. However, because the leopard is a large animal, living near rivers and lakes, it is not easy to hunt.

One day, while hunting, Tru Vuong saw a large prize, so he mirrored his bow and shot 4 arrows in a row. These four arrows all hit the prize and they got stuck on its body and on its shell.

After returning to the palace, to commemorate this meaningful hunt, Tru Vuong ordered the artisans to make a bronze statue in the shape of a peacock and cast four arrows on it. In addition, these artisans also received orders to engrave letters to tell about the hunting process of that day on the animal's carapace. The inscription on the bronze statue's shell shows that Tru Vuong was a king with a talent for archery.

This inscription and bronze statue is a living source of documents to help experts and researchers better understand the Shang Dynasty, which existed at least 3.000 years ago.

(Source: Vietnamese Women)
